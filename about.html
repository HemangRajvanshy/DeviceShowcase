Operation

The machine is operated in a manner similar to a typical exercise bike; the user will sit on the seat and rotate the pedals using their legs. As the pedals are rotated, they spin an axle that is connected to a system of gears, progressively increasing the RPM generated by the user by a significant amount. The system outputs its rotational motion to a belt that is connected to an electric dynamo. Electricity is then routed from the dynamo to an outlet located on the rear of the device.

The inner workings of the machine responsible for energy production will consist of mostly silver. Silver is very conductive due to its property of a higher number of movable atoms. Silver is also a cheaper and more effective conductor than many other metals. On top of all of this, a wide, high-quality biking seat will be attached to ensure comfort for long durations of usage. The make-up of our device creates a durable, long-lasting, and effective generator that has many different uses.

When an electrical device is plugged into the machine’s socket, the system will automatically detect its power consumption and display it on the screen in kilowatt-hours and Calories, in addition to the number of stairs/flights of stairs climbed and miles walked/ran that is equivalent to the energy usage. The display can also provide these quantities cumulatively, over the period the device was being used.

Follow The Energy

Since the primary purpose of designing this device is revealing its energy inputs to its user, it is equally important for them to also understand the transfer of energy that has to occur for this machine to operate and be functional. Let’s start following the path of the energy within this system when it is still in the form of calories in food. The chemical bonds within food are broken, converting the substances into more useful forms of energy for humans through a complex digestion process. One of the main purposes of digestion is to provide your cells with the necessary energy to operate through a chemical structure called ATP . Humans can utilize this energy to partake in many activities; however, within our machine, we attempt to harness humans’ ability to use their muscles to produce rotational motion by pushing pedals. The rotation of the Electricycle’s pedals will then turn a dynamo, producing electricity that may be stored, used, or transported elsewhere.

Some of this electric energy will be used to power the screen displaying information about current energy production, while the rest will be output through outlets on the bike’s rear. Any typical home battery can be plugged in and charged using the energy generated by the bike. This chain of energy transfers previously described is but only a fraction of the energy movements throughout the universe. Nevertheless, it serves to inform its users of the complex role energy plays in our lives.
