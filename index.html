Introduction

In our current era of technology, humans are more strongly connected to the latest electronics and most innovative gadgets than ever before. Paradoxically, despite this growth in overall technological exposure, many people still have difficulty understanding the nature of the energy that is utilized by the machinery of our modern world. As these individuals progress through their daily lives, they would rarely look upon the devices they use and wonder where exactly the energy required to power it arises from. One potential reason for this absence of curiosity is that modern electronics and machines obscure their energy inputs as opposed to revealing them to their user.

By simply looking at a house, it would be impossible to estimate its energy requirements because all of the piles, wires, switches, and controls are hidden under the ground and within the walls. Instead of keeping these energy inputs concealed, we have aspired to design a novel and practical product in the form of an power generating stationary bike that would illuminate its inputs to the user in an easily understandable fashion, while remaining completely functional.
